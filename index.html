Who We Are

Strengthening Clinical Resilience Across PICTs

The **Pacific Biomedical Engineering Network (PBEN)** forum operates directly under the guidance of the **Directors of Clinical Services (DCS)** and the **Pacific Heads of Health (HoH)**.

Core Objective of the Forum

PBEN provides a sovereign platform for the improvement of **coordination, collaboration, and capacity development** of its members. The overarching goal is to grant Pacific Island Countries & Territories (PICTs) full ownership of, and a collective mechanism to deliver inputs into, a regional biomedical engineering services development platform for the Pacific.

Standardizing policies, training, and maintenance frameworks.

Our Sacred Values

Foundational principles steering our biomedical network across boundaries.

Aroha (Care)

We deeply care about our communities. We take full, active ownership of our Biomedical Engineering work.

Unity

We progress together as a unified region. We leave no nation and no island community behind.

Excellence

We strive to be the absolute best. We mandate, practice, and deliver premium clinical safety standards.

Innovation

We continuously improve, stay resourceful, and evolve synchronously with rapid technological changes.

Our Vision

“Advanced biomedical engineering services for a healthier Blue Pacific”

Imagining a fully unified healthcare technology landscape across our expansive Pacific Ocean continent.

Our Mission

“To deliver innovative and sustainable biomedical engineering services in the Pacific by strengthening capacity building, policy development, regional collaboration and promotion of technologies.”

Guiding daily regional and national practices with operational standards and professional pathways.

Strategic Goals

“To strengthen biomedical engineering services in the Blue Pacific by ensuring equitable access to well-maintained medical equipment, supported by a qualified and competent biomedical workforce”

Our metric of success remains direct access of regional hospitals to sustainable diagnostics.

5 Thematic Pillars & Action Plan (2026 - 2031)

Referenced directly from the Strategic Blueprint "GEN_AI PBEN Strategic Plan 2026 - 2031.docx". Click through the pillars below to explore Key Priority Actions and Outcomes.

Pillar 1 Theme Statement

Advocacy & Awareness

Strategic Objective

Advocate for governments (through MOHs), donor partners and regional support, to prioritise and support BME in the Blue Pacific.

Key Priority Actions

  • Promote BME awareness through general education, media campaign and success stories.
  • Government to recognize standardized formal BME training and qualification.
  • Develop and maintain a regional BME network with members and external partners.
  • Advocate for dedicated national BME budgets for full lifecycle of medical equipment; seek donor and philanthropic funding.
  • Advocate to decrease carbon emissions and proper BME waste management.

Key Outcomes Expected

  • Increased visibility of BME.
  • Government recognized standard BME training and qualification.
  • Functional regional BME networking partnerships are in place.
  • Improved access to BME funding.
  • Decreased carbon emissions through establishment of BME waste management systems.
Pillar 2 Theme Statement

Workforce Development

Strategic Objective

Ensure a qualified, competent and dedicated BME workforce in the Blue Pacific.

Key Priority Actions

  • Set regional minimum qualifications and competency standards. For competency standards to include equipment certification and BMET workforce ratio to hospital beds and population needs.
  • BME to be recognized as a specialized clinical healthcare service.
  • To develop and improve workforce retention strategies ensuring adequate remuneration packages and career pathways at national levels for BME professions.
  • Strengthen BME surge capacity for health emergencies and mass gathering.

Key Outcomes Expected

  • Completed regional minimum qualifications and competency standards. Improved BME workforce.
  • BME recognized as a specialized field.
  • Endorsed Workforce Plans.
  • Improved BME systems' response to emergencies and mass gatherings.
Pillar 3 Theme Statement

Education & Training

Strategic Objective

Facilitate and improve access to BME education and training in the Blue Pacific.

Key Priority Actions

  • To explore and support certified training and education opportunities for BME.
  • Support provision of BME and end-users in-service training in health settings.
  • To support mentoring initiatives and strategies for capacity building opportunities, attachments, regional mentoring documentation etc.
  • Develop and strengthen a regional Continuous Professional Development (CPD) framework. Include CPD points, Train Of Trainer (TOT), conferences etc.

Key Outcomes Expected

  • Supported and updated certified training and education opportunities for BME.
  • Increased number of end-users and BME in-service training provided.
  • Mentoring initiatives and strategies for BME developed and supported.
  • CPD framework developed and strengthened.
Pillar 4 Theme Statement

Data & Research

Strategic Objective

Strengthen data collection, research and monitoring to inform decision-making.

Key Priority Actions

  • Develop and implement regional BME monitoring dashboard – database to standardize medical equipment, workforce etc.
  • To develop and implement a comprehensive equipment data management system for BME through a collaborated support mechanism.
  • Support BME research in the region.
  • To support and safeguard access, use and publication of BME data.

Key Outcomes Expected

  • Regional BME dashboard developed and implemented.
  • Comprehensive equipment data management system developed and implemented.
  • BME research supported.
  • Supported and safeguarded BME data.
Pillar 5 Theme Statement

Institutional Strengthening

Strategic Objective

Support PICTs to strengthen and develop improved robust biomedical structures, policies, governance and collaboration.

Key Priority Actions

  • Provide guidance on institutional development and policy frameworks.
  • Develop essential BME policy frameworks including but not limited to NMEM, MEMC, MED policies, BME procedure manual to inform decision-making and endorsement at the national level.
  • Support national efforts to strengthen BME governance.
  • Encourage institutional mutually beneficial regional collaboration.

Key Outcomes Expected

  • Endorsed institutional development and policy framework for BME.
  • Developed and strengthened essential BME policy.
  • Strengthened appropriate BME governance.
  • Increased institutional collaboration.
